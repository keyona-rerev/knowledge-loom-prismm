The landscape

The institution's position against the wealth transfer. Strengths and weaknesses are theirs. Opportunities and threats are the forces bearing down on them.

This is the analytical backbone of the page. The external row, opportunities and threats, is what content speaks to most. The standing threats are the shared pressure everyone feels. The triggered threats become the moment on each audience card below.
Internal, theirs to work with or fix

+Strengths

Decades-deep relationships with current account holders
Local trust and presence a neobank cannot fake
An existing wealth or trust arm in many institutions
A community and member ethos that makes serving families natural

−Weaknesses

Nobody owns the fact that an account holder died, no fix, no budget line
No relationship with the heirs, the next generation banks elsewhere
Beneficiary data is fragmented and manual
Runoff is invisible until the quarterly report, too late to act
Core-contract lock-in, only a year-five or six change window
External, the forces content speaks to

▲Opportunities

The $84T transfer is a one-time chance to win the next generation
Tools now exist to make the death-to-heir handoff a moment of care
Differentiate on care while everyone else competes on rate
Peer and league reference, one visible success lights up a cluster
A new leader's six-to-twelve-month mandate window

▼Threats

StandingThe transfer itself, deposits leave with the heirs, now, at scale
